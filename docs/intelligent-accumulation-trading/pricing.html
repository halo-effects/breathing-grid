Frequently Asked Questions

How is this different from the futures strategy? ‚ñº
Futures uses leverage and trades both long and short on perpetual contracts. Intelligent Accumulation is spot-only: you buy real coins in layers on dips and sell them in reverse order on recovery. No leverage, no funding fees, no liquidation risk. Typically 5‚Äì8√ó more profit per cycle due to scale-out exits.
What happens if a coin keeps dropping? ‚ñº
The system widens entry spacing in downtrends (regime detection), limits total safety orders per deal, and halts all entries in EXTREME conditions. Drawdown guardrails automatically de-risk the profile. Since you own the actual coin, you're accumulating at a discount ‚Äî when (not if) it recovers, scale-out captures that recovery.
Why is $3K the per-coin minimum? ‚ñº
DCA grids need sufficient depth to function. With a 4% base order and 8 safety orders doubling each time, the full grid requires ~255√ó the base order. At $3K minimum, each layer is large enough to cover exchange minimum order sizes and trading fees.
Can I run spot and futures simultaneously? ‚ñº
Yes ‚Äî the hybrid approach is recommended. Use spot for long-side accumulation (70% capital) and futures for short-side opportunities (30% capital). When the market drops, your shorts profit while your spot bot accumulates at lower prices. Natural hedge.
How does multi-DEX work? ‚ñº
Connect wallets to multiple DEXs (Aster, Hyperliquid, etc.). The bot manages positions across all of them from one dashboard. Your capital is spread across exchanges ‚Äî if one has issues, the rest continue running. Post-FTX, this is a feature, not just a technical detail.
What's the revenue model? ‚ñº
10% of your maximum capital tier, billed annually. A Pro tier ($25K max) costs ~$2,500/year. This aligns our incentives ‚Äî we only make more when you scale up, which means the system needs to perform for you first.
Do I need KYC? ‚ñº
Not for DEXs. Aster and Hyperliquid are wallet-based ‚Äî connect your wallet and trade. If you choose to add CEXs (Binance, Bybit, etc.) later, those require their own KYC, but the bot connects via API keys you control.
