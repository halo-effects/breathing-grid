Risk Analysis

What Drives Risk? The Amplifier Hierarchy

Understanding what affects your risk most ‚Üí least, and how each factor compounds the others.

1
Leverage (Highest Impact)
Multiplies every trade outcome proportionally ‚Äî 3√ó leverage means 3√ó gains AND 3√ó losses.
Direct position size amplification affects every single trade result and liquidation threshold.
2
SO Depth & Multiplier
Deeper Martingale = larger drawdowns before recovery, but faster recovery when trends reverse.
Each safety order compounds previous positions, creating exponential exposure growth.
3
Capital Reserve Reduction
Less buffer for unexpected volatility spikes, but higher capital utilization means more active opportunities.
Lower reserves = less ability to handle multiple simultaneous deals and margin volatility.
4
Extreme Regime Behavior
Trading in extreme volatility increases whipsaw losses but offers highest profit potential.
Affects 10-20% of trading days but creates disproportionate P&L impact ‚Äî both positive and negative.
5
Directional Bias Strength
Strong bias increases exposure to trend reversals but multiplies returns when direction is correct.
Higher bias = larger positions in trending direction. More effective in trending markets, dangerous in ranging.
6
TP/Deviation Tightness
Tighter parameters = more frequent but smaller trades, affecting win/loss ratio balance.
Fine-tuning for specific market conditions and volatility regimes ‚Äî lowest individual impact.

‚ö†Ô∏è Compound Risk Effects

Risk amplifiers multiply each other rather than simply adding. High leverage + deep SOs + low reserves = exponential risk increase. Conservative settings across all parameters = compound safety. This is why profile selection matters more than any single parameter tweak.
