Five Phases of the Wyckoff Lifecycle

Each phase has a clear objective, entry criteria, and exit triggers.

DCA — Accumulation

Grid trading in ranging markets. Small take-profit captures on each grid level. The default starting phase — always earning, even in sideways markets.

→ MARKUP: Breakout detected with trend confirmation
→ EXIT: Distribution signals fire at cycle top

MARKUP — Ride Uptrend

Capital deployed to ride the uptrend. Position size scales with your risk profile. Hold until the engine detects distribution.

→ EXIT: Distribution signals detected → begin position unwind

EXIT — Distribution

Methodical position unwind. Rally selling, trailing stops, and time-based urgency escalation protect your gains.

→ MARKDOWN: All positions unwound → capital rotates to other coins

MARKDOWN — Decline

Capital rotates to coins in earlier lifecycle phases (DCA or Spring). No idle cash — your portfolio stays productive while this coin’s market corrects.

→ SPRING: Significant price drop from exit → prepare for discounted re-entry
→ DCA: Price recovers (false exit) → return to grid

SPRING — Buy the Bottom

Tiered long deployment at 3 increasing discount levels from exit price. Each tier buys deeper into the capitulation, maximizing entry quality.

→ MARKUP: Price recovers with trend confirmation
→ DCA: Timeout with no recovery → fall back to grid

The Full Cycle

DCA accumulates → breakout triggers MARKUP → distribution signals EXIT → positions unwind, capital rotates to other coins during MARKDOWN → price drops trigger SPRING for discounted re-entry → recovery returns to MARKUP or timeout falls back to DCA.

Every bear market ends. Every bull market ends. The engine adapts.

MARKDOWN Phase Behavior

During MARKDOWN, the bot rotates capital to coins in DCA or Spring phases. Aggressiveness controls how quickly this rotation happens:

  • Conservative: Waits for full markdown confirmation before rotating. May catch a surprise Spring entry on the current coin.
  • Balanced: Moderate exit timing. Balances opportunity cost against premature exits.
  • Aggressive: Exits early on weakening signals. May miss a Spring but redeploys capital faster elsewhere.
